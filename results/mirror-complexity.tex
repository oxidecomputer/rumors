\section{The system, in one page}

Each replica holds a \textbf{sparse Merkle radix trie}: branching factor
256, fixed depth 32, one path byte per level (\code{src/tree.rs:18-26},
\code{src/tree/typed/height.rs:110-117}). A leaf's 32-byte path is
$\mathrm{blake3}\bigl(\mathrm{blake3}(\textit{version}) \,\Vert\,
\mathrm{blake3}(\textit{value})\bigr)$ --- the \emph{version is folded into
the path} (\code{src/tree/typed/path.rs:32-44}), so every insert, even of
identical content, lands at a fresh, computationally-uniform position.
Single-child runs are path-compressed into a prefix byte string on the node
(\code{src/tree/typed/untyped.rs:25-47}, invariant ``branches have $\ge 2$
children'' at \code{untyped.rs:78-81, 128-150}). Each node lazily memoizes
its subtree hash (\code{untyped.rs:266-288}) and its version ceiling/floor
(\code{untyped.rs:298-360}); the memos survive copy-on-write clones
(\code{untyped.rs:100-123}). Hashing is compression-invariant: a compressed
prefix byte wraps the child hash exactly as a materialized one-child branch
would (\code{src/tree/typed/hash.rs:54-79}, \code{untyped.rs:268-287}).
Leaves hash to a constant (\code{hash.rs:45-51}) --- all leaf-distinguishing
content lives in the \emph{path}.

The \textbf{mirror protocol} reconciles two replicas' trees over a wire.
After a 25-byte raw preamble (\code{src/tree/traverse/mirror/remote.rs:11-17})
and a framed version handshake, equal versions terminate immediately
(\code{src/tree/traverse/mirror/local.rs:230-241, 268-281};
\code{remote.rs:279-285}). Otherwise both sides run a synchronized descent.
Each side keeps a \emph{zipper} of per-height levels
(\code{src/tree/typed/levels.rs}); each message a side sends descends its
own zipper by \textbf{two heights} (\code{local.rs:395-415};
\code{partition.rs:354-356}), and because the two sides alternate strictly,
the conversation's frontier advances \textbf{one depth per message, two per
round trip}.

Each steady-state message carries three channels
(\code{src/tree/traverse/mirror/}\allowbreak\code{message.rs:50-70},
asymmetry matrix at \code{local.rs:46-58}):

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}L{0.14\textwidth}L{0.32\textwidth}L{0.44\textwidth}@{}}
\toprule
Channel & Content & Receiver's action \\
\midrule
\code{uncertain} &
$(\textit{prefix}, \textit{hash})$ of the sender's disputed-frontier
subtrees &
merge-join against own children: equal hash $\Rightarrow$ drop; differ
$\Rightarrow$ explode grandchildren (recurse); we-lack $\Rightarrow$
\code{request}; they-lack $\Rightarrow$ \code{provide} \\
\addlinespace
\code{requested} &
prefixes the sender lacks entirely &
answer next message by exploding that node's children into \code{providing}
(\code{partition.rs:91-142}) \\
\addlinespace
\code{providing} &
whole subtrees, full structure on the wire (\code{message.rs:50-58}) &
insert at the named prefix (\code{partition.rs:57-85}) \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

All per-round set operations are single linear merges over sorted flat
vectors (\code{partition.rs:}\allowbreak\code{100-115, 199-203};
\code{src/tree/typed/}\allowbreak\code{levels/level.rs}), so per-round work
is linear in the
frontier and message sizes --- no hidden log factors beyond the \code{imbl}
\code{OrdMap} child maps (an $O(\log 256)$ constant per child operation,
absorbed below).

\textbf{Termination is in-band}: a side is done when its outgoing
\code{requested} and \code{uncertain} are both empty
(\code{partition.rs:393-410}; \code{remote.rs:34-43, 364-374, 398-409}); a
sender that just emitted an empty-empty message does not even await a reply
(\code{remote.rs:398-409}). The schedule has a hard ceiling ---
\code{mirror\_connected} (\code{src/tree/traverse/mirror.rs:126-137}) is
\code{initiator} + \code{responder} + \code{open\_initiator} + a \code{seq!}
loop of \textbf{14 exchange pairs} + \code{close\_initiator} +
\code{complete\_responder} + \code{complete\_initiator}. Counting frames:
2 handshake + \code{Initiate} (depth 0) + \code{Opening} (depth 1) +
\code{Exchange} (depth 2) + 28 exchanges (depths 3--30) + one more responder
exchange (depth 31) + \code{Closing} + \code{Complete} = \textbf{36 frames
maximum}, covering all 32 byte-levels (the chain lengths are pinned at the
type level in \code{src/tree/traverse/mirror/protocol.rs:477-488}).

\textbf{Deletions} leave no tombstones. Every subtree shipped through
\code{providing} is first filtered by \code{Unknown::unknown}
(\code{src/tree/traverse/unknown.rs}): a subtree whose version
\emph{ceiling} is $\le$ the counterparty's version vector is something they
have already seen --- its absence on their side means they deleted it --- so
it is dropped in $O(1)$ (one memoized-ceiling comparison,
\code{unknown.rs:28-34}), \emph{and the local copy is dropped too} (the
Left arm keeps a node only if it survives the filter,
\code{partition.rs:232-239}). A floor check keeps wholly-unknown subtrees
intact without rebuilding them (\code{unknown.rs:38-52}).

\section{Model and assumptions}

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{(A1) Uniform leaf placement.} Leaf paths are BLAKE3 outputs
  over $(\textit{version}, \textit{value})$ (\code{path.rs:32-44}), and
  versions are unique per action (\code{src/tree.rs:299-318}). In the
  random-oracle model the $N$ leaf paths are i.i.d.\ uniform 32-byte
  strings, \emph{regardless of insertion order, batching, or payload
  skew} --- an adversary would need structured BLAKE3 preimages to cluster
  them. This is the load-bearing probabilistic assumption; see \S9.4.
\item \textbf{(A2) Parameters.} Branching $b = 256$, depth 32
  (\code{tree.rs:21}, \code{height.rs:110-117}). Hash size $h = 32$ bytes
  (\code{hash.rs:17}).
\item \textbf{(A3) Warm memos.} Subtree hashes and version bounds are
  memoized reads (\code{untyped.rs:266-288}); we count a hash
  \emph{comparison} as $O(1)$. First-touch costs after local mutation are a
  separate, additive term (\S9.1) --- the benches warm them in untimed
  setup (\code{benches/gossip\_grid.rs:119-160}, \code{src/tree.rs:182-196}).
\item \textbf{(A4) Dispute drives descent.} Only the
  \emph{Both-and-hashes-differ} cell of the asymmetry matrix recurses
  (\code{partition.rs:243-254}). The Left cell ships a whole subtree in the
  same message; the Right cell is requested and answered whole one message
  later (\code{partition.rs:91-142}). So one-sided differences cost $O(1)$
  additional messages once they separate from two-sided content.
\item \textbf{(A5) Two heights per send, one depth per message.} Verified
  at \code{local.rs:395-415} and the alternation in \code{mirror.rs:126-137}.
\item \textbf{(A6) No level skipping.} Path compression does \textbf{not}
  shorten the descent: exploding a compressed node pops exactly one prefix
  byte and yields a singleton child map (\code{untyped.rs:156-167}).
  Compression saves hashing and memory, never rounds.
\item \textbf{(A7) No hash collisions.} A false \code{uncertain} match
  (probability $\le \textit{pairs} \cdot 2^{-256}$) would silently prune;
  ignored.
\item \textbf{(A8) Wire sizes.} An \code{uncertain} entry at depth $k$ is
  $k$ raw prefix bytes + 32 hash bytes (\code{prefix.rs:127-156},
  \code{message.rs:61-63}); a \code{providing} node is its full borsh
  structure: per leaf, a version, the payload, and $\le 32$
  compressed-prefix bytes (\code{message.rs:21-58},
  \code{untyped.rs:401-438}). Frames carry a 4-byte length prefix
  (\code{remote.rs:26-33}).
\end{itemize}

\section{Warm-up: \texorpdfstring{$D = 1$, $N = 10^6$}{D = 1, N = 10\textasciicircum 6}}

One side holds a single extra leaf; the trees otherwise agree on $10^6$
messages. Trace the protocol:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Handshake} (2 frames): versions differ, descend.
\item \textbf{\code{Initiate}} (depth 0): one root hash. Differs.
\item \textbf{\code{Opening}} (depth 1): the responder \emph{unconditionally}
  explodes its root and lists all its children (\code{local.rs:333-353}) ---
  at $N = 10^6$, all 256 slots are occupied ($\approx 3{,}906$ leaves each):
  \textbf{256 entries}.
\item The initiator merge-joins: 255 child hashes match and drop; the one
  slot containing the extra leaf differs (Both-case), so its 256
  grandchildren are exploded and listed: \textbf{256 entries} at depth 2.
\item The responder finds one depth-2 hash differing; that node has
  $\approx 10^6/256^2 \approx 15$ occupied children:
  \textbf{$\approx$ 15 entries} at depth 3.
\item At depth 3 the extra leaf sits alone in its slot with probability
  $\approx 1 - N/256^3 \approx 0.94$: a \textbf{Left case}. The whole leaf
  (a path-compressed spine node carrying its remaining $\approx 29$ path
  bytes, version, and payload) ships in \code{providing}; nothing is
  exploded; the outgoing \code{uncertain} and \code{requested} are empty
  $\Rightarrow$ \textbf{Done}, in-band.
\end{enumerate}

Totals: $\approx 256 + 256 + 15 \approx 527$ hash entries on the wire and
compared (${\sim}18$\,KB), 7--8 frames $\approx$ 4 round trips including the
preamble, one message body. Three things to notice, which the general
analysis formalizes:

\begin{itemize}
\item The \emph{visited disputed nodes} number only $\approx 3$ (one per
  level until separation) --- $D \cdot (1 + \log_b(N/D))$ with $D = 1$.
\item But each visited node costs its \textbf{full sibling fan} ($\le 256$
  entries): the per-node constant is $f_k \approx \min(b, N/b^k)$. This
  factor-256 is the price of the radix-256 tradeoff: few rounds, fat rounds.
\item Separation happened at depth
  $\approx \log_b(D \cdot N) = \log_b 10^6 \approx 2.5$ --- not
  $\log_b N$ levels of \emph{messages saved}, because termination is the
  in-band emptiness predicate, not bottoming out at depth 32.
\end{itemize}

\section{Two lemmas}

\begin{lemma}[prefix occupancy / sharing]
Throw $m$ uniform leaves into the $b^k$ bins at depth $k$. The expected
number of occupied bins is $b^k \cdot (1 - (1 - b^{-k})^m)$, which is
$\Theta(\min(b^k, m))$ --- at least $(1 - 1/e) \cdot \min(b^k, m)$, at most
$\min(b^k, m)$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
Linearity of expectation over bins; $(1 - 1/x)^x \le 1/e$. Applied to the
$D$ differing leaves, this bounds how many distinct ancestors they have at
each depth: their root-to-leaf paths share prefixes exactly while
$b^k < D$, and are essentially disjoint below. Summing, the union of the
$D$ differing root-to-separation paths has
$\sum_k \min(b^k, D) = \Theta(D \cdot (1 + \log_b(N/D)))$ distinct nodes
(the geometric head contributes $\Theta(D)$; the plateau contributes $D$
per level from $j_D$ to the dispute cutoff of Lemma~2).
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[dispute persistence]
A depth-$k$ prefix is disputed --- i.e.\ recursed into rather than
resolved --- iff it contains a differing leaf \emph{and} the counterparty
owns at least one leaf under it (A4: otherwise it is a Left/Right case and
resolves in $O(1)$ messages). Under A1,
\begin{equation*}
\mathbb{E}[\Delta_k] \;\le\; \min\bigl( b^k,\; D,\; 2 \cdot D \cdot N \cdot b^{-k} \bigr)
\end{equation*}
and $\Pr(\text{any dispute survives depth } k) \le 2 \cdot D \cdot N \cdot b^{-k}$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
The first two bounds are Lemma~1 applied to the differing leaves. For the
third: a dispute at depth $k$ requires some differing leaf $d$ and some
leaf $x$ on the side opposite $d$'s holder to share a $k$-byte prefix. Each
such (ordered) pair shares it with probability $b^{-k}$ (uniform,
independent paths), and there are at most
$D_L \cdot (C + D_R) + D_R \cdot (C + D_L) \le 2 \cdot D \cdot N$ pairs.
Union bound / linearity.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}
$k^* \le \log_b(2 \cdot D \cdot N) + t$ with probability $\ge 1 - b^{-t}$;
$\mathbb{E}[k^*] \le \log_b(2DN) + \frac{b}{b-1} \cdot \frac{1}{\ln b}
\approx \log_b(2DN) + 1$. The fixed 32-level ceiling is unreachable
probabilistically: hitting it would need $D \cdot N \approx 256^{30}$. (It
exists because paths are 32 bytes; even a full-depth descent is
\emph{correct} --- equal full paths imply equal
$(\textit{version}, \textit{value})$ by construction, \code{tree.rs:22-26}.)
\end{corollary}

\begin{lemma}[fan-out]
A depth-$k$ node in the union tree of $N$ uniform leaves has expected
occupied-children count $f_k = \Theta(\min(b, \max(1, N/b^k)))$, and the
\emph{total} children over any set of $\Delta$ depth-$k$ nodes is
$\le \Delta \cdot b$ always and $\Theta(\Delta \cdot f_k)$ in expectation
(children counts across disjoint subtrees are negatively associated; we
only ever need linearity). \emph{Caution (Jensen):}
$\mathbb{E}[\min(X, Y)] \le \min(\mathbb{E}X, \mathbb{E}Y)$, so products of
the $\min$ envelopes used below are upper envelopes of expectations; the
matching lower bounds hold within the $(1 - 1/e)$ constants of Lemma~1.
\end{lemma}

\section{The level-by-level sum}

Per round at depth $k$, the side processing the counterparty's
\code{uncertain} does (all single-pass, sorted merges ---
\code{partition.rs:159-304}):

\begin{itemize}
\item drain its frontier once: $O(|\textit{frontier}_k|)$;
\item for each disputed parent, merge-join its own occupied children
  against the listed hashes: $O(\text{own fan} + \text{listed entries})$,
  each comparison a memoized-hash \code{memcmp} (A3);
\item explode each hash-differing child's grandchildren into the next
  level and emit them as the next \code{uncertain}
  (\code{partition.rs:243-254, 364-373}).
\end{itemize}

So the entries \emph{listed at depth $k$} are exactly the occupied children
of the disputed depth-$(k-1)$ parents, and each side's per-session work,
the total \code{uncertain} traffic, and the zipper/\code{collapse} churn
(\code{levels.rs:148-197}, linear in total level entries) are all $\Theta$
of the same master sum:
\begin{equation*}
W \;=\; \sum_{k=0}^{32} \Delta_k \cdot f_k
\;\approx\; \sum_k \min\bigl(b^k,\, D,\, 2DN \cdot b^{-k}\bigr)
            \cdot \min\bigl(b,\, \max(1,\, N/b^k)\bigr)
\end{equation*}

Evaluate by regimes (write $j_D = \log_b D$, $j_N = \log_b N$; assume first
$D \le N/b^2$ so all three regimes are non-empty):

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}L{0.30\textwidth}lL{0.13\textwidth}L{0.26\textwidth}@{}}
\toprule
Regime & Depths & Term & Contribution \\
\midrule
\textbf{Head} (paths shared) & $k \le j_D$ & $b^k \cdot b$ &
  geometric, $\approx b \cdot D \cdot (1 + 1/b + \dots) \approx b \cdot D$ \\
\addlinespace
\textbf{Plateau} (paths distinct, fans saturated) & $j_D < k \le j_N - 1$ &
  $D \cdot b$ & $b \cdot D \cdot (\log_b(N/D) - 1)$ \\
\addlinespace
\textbf{Tail} (fans thin out, disputes die) & $k > j_N - 1$ &
  $D \cdot N \cdot b^{-k} \cdot O(1)$ &
  geometric, $\approx \Theta(D \cdot b)$ at the shoulder, then $\div 256$
  per level \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\begin{theorem}[per-side computation]
Under A1--A6, the expected per-side work (hash comparisons + level-entry
handling, each $O(1)$) and the expected total \code{uncertain} entry count
are
\begin{equation*}
W \;=\; \Theta\bigl( \min(b \cdot D,\, N) \cdot (1 + \log_b(N/D)) \bigr)
\end{equation*}
\begin{itemize}
\item for $D \le N/b$: $W = \Theta(b \cdot D \cdot (1 + \log_b(N/D)))$ ---
  the prompt-level form $\Theta(D \cdot (1 + \log(N/D)))$ counts
  \emph{disputed nodes visited}; the protocol pays a $\times b$ sibling fan
  on each (Lemma~3, \S4);
\item for $N/b < D \le N$ (heavily diverged / disjoint): the plateau
  vanishes and the peak term is $\Theta(N)$;
  $W = \Theta(N) = \Theta(D) \cdot O(b)$ --- linear;
\item additive $\Theta(D \cdot (1 + s_v))$ for absorbing/filtering the
  provided subtrees and the final collapse, and $\Theta(1)$ per round of
  fixed pipeline cost. \qed
\end{itemize}
\end{theorem}

Cross-checks: $D = 1$, $N = 10^6$ gives
$W \approx 256 \cdot (1 + \log_b(N/b)) \approx 640$ vs.\ the \S4 hand count
of 527 \checkmark. Disjoint trees ($C = 0$, $N = D$) give $\Theta(N)$
\checkmark{} (matches the one-shot character of bootstrap, \S9.2).

\begin{theorem}[bandwidth]
Total bytes on the wire, both directions (each depth's \code{uncertain} is
listed by exactly one side --- the alternation splits levels between them):
\begin{align*}
\textit{bytes} \;=\;& W \cdot (h + \bar{k})
  && \text{[\code{uncertain}: 32-byte hash + $k$-byte prefix, $\bar{k} \le j_N + 1$]} \\
  &+ \Theta(D) \cdot (\bar{s} + s_v + {\le}32)
  && \text{[\code{providing}: payloads, versions, compressed spines]} \\
  &+ O(D) \cdot \bar{k}
  && \text{[\code{requested}: one prefix per separated they-only subtree]} \\
  &+ {\le}\, 36 \cdot 4 + 2 \cdot 25 + 2 \cdot s_v
  && \text{[framing, preamble, handshake]}
\end{align*}
In $\Theta$-terms:
$\Theta(D \cdot (\bar{s} + s_v))
 + \Theta(\min(b \cdot D, N) \cdot (1 + \log_b(N/D)) \cdot h)$.
The empirical ``${\sim}D$'' is right in its $D$-scaling; the per-$D$
constant is the interesting part: at $N/D = 10^2 \dots 10^4$ the hash
channel runs ${\sim}10$--$40$\,KB \emph{per differing message} (e.g.\ \S4:
18\,KB to move one message). For small payloads, \textbf{hash traffic
dominates bandwidth}; bodies dominate only when
$\bar{s} \gtrsim 36 \cdot W/D$ bytes. \qed
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[latency]
Messages strictly alternate and the conversation advances one depth per
message (A5). Disputes empty at depth $k^*$ (Lemma~2 corollary), plus
$O(1)$ messages to flush the final
$\code{requested} \to \code{providing}$ exchange; the in-band predicate
then stops the session (A6 --- no draining of the remaining schedule):
\begin{equation*}
\mathrm{RTT} \;=\; \tfrac{1}{2} \cdot k^* + O(1)
\;=\; \tfrac{1}{2} \cdot \log_{256}(2 \cdot D \cdot N) + c,
\qquad c \approx 3\text{--}4
\end{equation*}
(preamble $\approx 1$, handshake 1, \code{Initiate}/\code{Opening} 1,
closing tail ${\sim}1$), hard-ceilinged at 36 frames $\approx$ 18 RTT,
floored at ${\sim}2$ RTT total for equal versions. Path compression does
not reduce this (A6). Numerically the \emph{descent} portion is tiny at any
scale --- 1.8 levels $\approx$ 1 RTT for $(N, D) = (10^6, 1)$ up to 6.1
levels $\approx$ 3 RTT for $(10^7, 10^7)$; the $O(1)$ dominates. The
empirical $\log_{256}(C + D)$ agrees at $D \approx N$ (where
$\tfrac{1}{2} \log DN = \log N$) and is $\approx 2\times$ the true descent
depth for $D \ll N$, a difference of $< 2$ RTT at any realistic scale ---
unfalsifiable from timing, trivially checkable by counting frames (\S8). \qed
\end{theorem}

\section{Against the \texorpdfstring{$\sqrt{C \cdot D}$}{sqrt(C·D)} fit}

\begin{proposition}[the fit is a true upper bound]
For $1 \le D \le C$:
\begin{equation*}
D \cdot \ln(C/D) \;\le\; \frac{2}{e} \cdot \sqrt{C \cdot D},
\qquad \text{with equality iff } D = C/e^2.
\end{equation*}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Set $x = D/C \in (0, 1]$ and maximize $g(x) = \sqrt{x} \cdot \ln(1/x)$:
$g'(x) = (\ln(1/x) - 2)/(2\sqrt{x}) = 0$ at $x = e^{-2}$,
$g(e^{-2}) = 2/e$. Then
$D \ln(C/D) = C \cdot x \ln(1/x) = C\sqrt{x} \cdot g(x) \cdots$ ---
directly,
$D \cdot \ln(C/D) = \sqrt{CD} \cdot \sqrt{x} \cdot \ln(1/x)
\le (2/e)\sqrt{CD}$.
\end{proof}

In base 256:
$D \cdot \log_{256}(C/D) \le (2/(e \cdot \ln 256)) \cdot \sqrt{CD}
\approx 0.133 \cdot \sqrt{CD}$. With Theorem~1's constants
($b/\ln b \approx 46.2$), for $D \le C$:
\begin{equation*}
W \;\le\; b \cdot D \cdot (2 + \ln 2)
        + \frac{2}{e} \cdot \frac{b}{\ln b} \cdot \sqrt{CD}
\;\approx\; 690 \cdot D + 34 \cdot \sqrt{C \cdot D}
\;=\; O(\sqrt{C \cdot D}),
\end{equation*}
since $D \le \sqrt{CD}$ when $D \le C$. So the empirical
$\sqrt{C \cdot D}$ is a \emph{correct} upper bound on per-side
computation --- but loose: the ratio
$\sqrt{CD}/(D \cdot \log(C/D)) = \sqrt{C/D}/\log(C/D)$ grows without bound
as $D/C \to 0$, and the bound is tight (constant-factor) only in the band
$D/C \in [{\sim}10^{-2}, {\sim}\tfrac{1}{2}]$ where $\sqrt{x} \cdot \ln(1/x)$
stays within $1.5\times$ of its max.

\paragraph{Why the fit nevertheless matched the data: the staircase.}
The smooth form $b \cdot D \cdot (1 + \log_b(N/D))$ is the envelope of a
\emph{staircase}: each level of the sum spans a factor of $256 \approx 2.4$
decades. Inside one span the dominant term is $t_k = D \cdot N/b^k$ ---
\textbf{linear in $N$ at fixed $D$} --- and the term above it,
$\min(b, D) \cdot b^k$-shaped, is \emph{flat} in $D$ once $D > b$. The
exact sum's local log-log slopes therefore wobble around the sqrt's 0.5
instead of sitting at the asymptotic 0 (in $N$) and 1 (in $D$):

\paragraph{Conclusion.} $\sqrt{C \cdot D}$ and
$\Theta(\min(bD, N)(1 + \log_b(N/D)))$ are empirically indistinguishable
from wall time on diagonal or $D$-sweeps at grid scales; they separate
cleanly on \textbf{fixed-$D$, $N$-sweeps across the $10^5 \to 10^6$
decade}, and on any \emph{count}-based (rather than time-based)
measurement.

\section{An experiment that distinguishes them}

All using existing infrastructure; no protocol changes.

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Byte counting (noise-free, decisive).} In
  \code{benches/gossip\_grid.rs}, wrap the
  \code{PipeReader}/\code{PipeWriter} ends of \code{Wire}
  (\code{gossip\_grid.rs:43-99}) in trivial
  \code{std::io::Read}/\code{Write} adapters that increment
  \code{AtomicU64}s, and record bytes per session per cell. Bandwidth is
  $\Theta(36 \cdot W + \text{body terms})$ by Theorem~2 and is exactly
  measurable. Regress $\log(\textit{bytes} - a \cdot D)$ on $\log D$ and
  $\log(N/D)$ over cells with $D \ge 100$: the sqrt model predicts
  elasticity $\approx 0.5$ in $\log(N/D)$; Theorem~1 predicts $\approx 0$
  (a log-of-log). Frame \emph{counts} from the same adapter (count 4-byte
  headers) verify Theorem~3 against
  $\tfrac{1}{2} \cdot \log_{256}(2DN) + c$ to $\pm 1$ frame.
\item \textbf{The flat decade.} Fix $\textit{differing} = 1{,}000$,
  $\textit{redacted} = 0$, sweep
  $\textit{common} \in \{10^3, 10^4, 10^5\}$ (cells already exist; extend
  \code{COMMON} in \code{benches/support/grid.rs:74} to $10^6$ for the
  decisive decade, accepting the 10-sample floor). Predicted wall-time
  ratios $\textit{common } 10^5 \to 10^6$: sqrt $\times 3.16$; Theorem~1
  $\times 1.2$ (the $0.08$-slope decade). One afternoon of Criterion time.
\item \textbf{Direct hash-compare counts.} Drive two
  \code{local::Exchange}s in-process through the \code{\#[cfg(test)]}
  \code{mirror::mirror} entry (\code{mirror.rs:337-353}) with grid-shaped
  trees and count \code{uncertain} entries (e.g.\ instrument in a test, or
  just count from the byte totals:
  $\textit{entries} \approx (\textit{uncertain bytes})/(34 \dots 64)$).
  This measures $W$ itself, bypassing the $\alpha \cdot D + \textit{fixed}$
  wall-time floor entirely.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Caveats and edge regimes}

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Lazy memoization (first session after writes).} A batch of
  $m$ local changes leaves cold \code{OnceLock}s on the
  $\Theta(m \cdot (1 + \log_b(N/m)))$ ancestor nodes of the changed paths
  (\code{untyped.rs:40-47}; memos survive \code{Arc}-sharing, so
  \emph{only} changed paths are cold, \code{untyped.rs:100-123}). The
  first \code{hash()} then pays one branch preimage per cold node --- up
  to $1 + 33 \cdot 256 \approx 8.4$\,KB of BLAKE3 for a saturated branch,
  but only 34 bytes per compressed-spine level (\code{untyped.rs:283-287};
  both conventions measured in \code{benches/hash\_recon.rs}). This cost
  is additive, amortizes across sessions, and is excluded from the benches
  by \code{warm\_caches()} (\code{gossip\_grid.rs:154-160}).
\item \textbf{Bootstrap / one side empty ($D = N$).} No prefix has content
  on both sides, so there are \emph{zero} disputes: the empty responder
  sends an empty \code{Opening} (\code{local.rs:337-341}), and the
  initiator's root-level drain Left-provides every root child in one
  message (\code{partition.rs:276-294}), filtered through \code{Unknown}
  against the empty version (everything survives). Cost: $\Theta(N)$
  transfer and absorption, $O(1)$ rounds. Theorem~1's formula degrades
  gracefully to $\Theta(N)$ here, but the \emph{mechanism} is different
  --- the dispute machinery never engages.
\item \textbf{Deletion honoring.} A redacted leaf (present on A, deleted
  on B) keeps hashes differing along its path, so it costs descent exactly
  like a differing leaf --- \textbf{count redactions in $D$} (the grid's
  throughput accounting already does: \code{grid.rs:115-127}). But it
  costs no body bytes: at separation, A's Left-provide is killed by the
  $O(1)$ ceiling check (\code{unknown.rs:28-34}) and A deletes its own
  copy (\code{partition.rs:232-239}) --- deletion \emph{propagates}
  through the version ceiling, with no tombstone and no transfer. A whole
  missing-but-dominated subtree dies the same way: one memoized
  comparison, no descent below it.
\item \textbf{Skew.} A1 is enforced by content addressing, not hoped for:
  because the version is folded into the leaf path (\code{path.rs:32-44}),
  even a million-message single-party version-batch spreads uniformly ---
  concentration under few prefixes would require engineered BLAKE3
  outputs. (Contrast key-ordered Merkle trees, where a contiguous batch
  \emph{does} cluster.) Even granting an adversary clustered paths, the
  depth ceiling caps the damage at $W \le 32 \cdot b \cdot D$ and 18 RTT.
\item \textbf{Version sizes.} ITC \code{Version}s are not constant-size:
  they ride the handshake, every provided leaf, and every
  \code{ceiling/floor} $\le$ comparison (\code{unknown.rs:31, 46-49}),
  contributing the $s_v$ factors in Theorems~1--2. For long-lived
  universes with many party splits, $s_v$ is the term to watch, orthogonal
  to the tree combinatorics.
\item \textbf{The radix constant is the design tradeoff.} Per disputed
  node the protocol ships a full sibling fan (up to $256 \times 36$ish
  bytes $\approx$ 9\,KB) to buy a 2.4-decade depth reduction per level.
  $b = 256$ puts every realistic deployment within ${\sim}3$ levels of
  separation (RTT-optimal) at the cost of the $\times b$ in $W$ --- which
  is exactly the constant that made a $\sqrt{C \cdot D}$ fit land in the
  right numeric range in the first place.
\end{enumerate}
